Federal Writersí Project
Research, Interview, and Writing Assignment

Teacher directions: Pass the below assignment sheet out after you have talked about the New Deal programs.  Read over the first page with your class.  Make the first assignment, which is topic brainstorming.  This writing assignment is set-up so students can pick topics from the 1930s on through the 1970s.  However, it can also be done where the time period is limited to the New Deal era.

Introductory Information: One of FDRís New Deal programs was the Workers Progress Administration (WPA).  The WPA employed 35 million unemployed people to build or improve schools, airports and roads.  Within the WPA there were divisions that hired writers, actors, artists, and musicians to bring the performing arts to people in many U.S. communities.  Another division of the WPA was called the Federal Writersí Project (FWP).  The FWP hired people to interview those people who had been slaves before the Civil War and to write down the oral histories of what slave life was like.  Many of these former-slaves had experience slavery only as children and were in their 70s and 80s when the interviews were held.  What resulted from the efforts of the FWP was an invaluable reserve of primary source information.

Directions: Each student will be taking on a role similar to that of those who worked for the FWP.  For the purpose of this writing assignment, the student will be interviewing a person of their choosing on any one of a variety of historic events that have occurred between 1930s and 1980s.  The final result of each student effort will be a historic primary source document, which will be compiled into a collection with other similar student produced documents to be utilized by future students.  These papers will possibly be set up on a web page or in a book.
 This writing assignment will be completed through several stages over a 22 day period (number of days may vary depending on when weekends fall, test dates and other things).  Many steps of this assignment have a point value attached to them.  The student is to keep this handout and record their points on it.

Note: The teacher reserves the right to make any necessary changes/adaptions to the below schedule.
FWP Writing Schedule

Stage 1: Pre-writing

 Step 1: Brainstorming - students will look through books and talk to parents about possible subject topics and possible people to interview.  Note: the person being interviewed must have experienced the subject they are telling about first-hand.  Examples would be a someone who attended Woodstock or someone who fought in WWII.  Class time may be given for students to share their ideas.  A list of ideas and people must be submitted.

Date due:           Time required: 2 days    Points earned:     /
 

 Step 2: Topic and interviewee selection - student is to turn in a piece of paper listing the topic they choose and who they will be interviewing and WHEN.  The interview date and time should be convenient for the person being interviewed. A this point, there is about one week until interviews are to be held.  Interviews should be held after pre-research and question creations steps are completed (so in about 3-5 days from when this step in completed.

Date due:           Time required: 2 days    Points earned:     /
 

Stage 2: Information Collection

 Step 1: Pre-research - student researches their topic.  Time may be given for in-class research.  A short, informal write-up of the research and list of sources is to be submitted.

Date due:           Time required: 2 days   Points earned:     /
 

 Step 2: Question Creation - student is to come up with 5-10 good questions to ask during the interview.  The questions must be submitted to the teacher. In class time may be given.

Date due:           Time required: 2 days    Points earned:     /
 

 Step 3: Interviewing - student is to a conduct the interview at the arranged time. Students should listen and let their subject talk freely, but should also get the answers to their questions. Students should arrive on time, be polite and thank the person, bring their list of questions, paper and pen, and tape recorder (if possible).  Student should take detailed notes and try to get useable quotes.

Date due:            Time required: 4 days   Points earned:
 Step 4: Post-research - student does further research to fill in gaps and make sense of information gathered during the interview.  Time may be given for in-class research.  Any additional research information and new sources are to be written down and submitted to the teacher.  If none, a note saying so is to be submitted.

Date Due:          Time required: 2 days     Points earned:     /
 

Stage 3: Drafting

 Step 1: First Draft - student writes the paper.  It must be at least 2 pages, be typed, double spaced, in 12 pt Courier (or similar).  Quotes and general information for the interview should dominate the paper, but information gathered from book research can also be used to a limited degree.  A bibliography of books researched and the person interviewed should be included using a consisted citation format.

Date Due:          Time required: 4 days     Points earned:     /
 

 Step 2: Writerís Workshop - in groups of three, students take turns reading their papers to each other.  Students are to self-correct and edit their papers as they read while the other two students are to listen and make suggestions.

Date Due:          Time required: 1 day     Points earned:     /
 

 Step 3: Final Draft - student makes corrections and necessary additions, and other final touches.  The final draft must also be typed, double spaced, in 12 pt Courier (or similar).  It is to be free of errors in spelling, grammar, and sentence structure.  Must have a nice cover page with ìFederal Writersí Projectî at top (or whatever title the class or teacher decides on).  In the middle of the page should be listed the studentís name, paper title, ìas told by [name of person interviewed], semester, class, teacherís name.

Date Due:          Time required: 3 days    Points earned:     /
