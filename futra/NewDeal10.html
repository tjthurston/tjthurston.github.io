Court Packing Cartoons
Small Group Activity

Directions: You will want to look through the FDR Cartoon Database  for cartoons to use.  Find at least one cartoon that supports FDR and at least one that is against the FDRís court packing.  No doubt, it will be easier to find ones against the plan.  Then, make 5-6 copies of the cartoons and the analysis guide and staple them into packets.  Divide your class into groups of 4-5.  Give each group a packet.  Allow them about 15 minutes to look over the cartoons and interpret them.  If you want to, and if you find enough cartoons, have each group analyze their own cartoon and then explain what they found to the entire class.  Make sure, however, that each group has copies of ALL of the cartoons in their packets. This way, they wonít get distracted so easily while the other groups are presenting their findings.  Their is a good Cartoon Analysis guide  in Social Education in a 1997 issue.  I donít know which issue for it was forwarded to me without the issue information.  If you happen to know, please e-mail me with it and I will add that information to this lesson plan.

Cartoon Analysis:
Identify any symbols:  these could be people, places, slogans, numbers, or symbols.
Identify any people in the cartoon.  Who are they and why are they important.
Identify where the cartoon is taking place.  Is this significant?
Identify any words or slogans.  What do they mean.
Identify the artists attitude.  Does the artist seem to be making a positive or negative statement.
Identify any charactitures.  These would include any body/facial features that might be exaggerated to bring attention for them.  Are they positive or negative?
