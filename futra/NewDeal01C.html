Instructional Objectives

At the conclusion of this lesson, which includes a variety of discussions and primary sources (photos, political cartoons and documents) the middle or high school students in your class will be able to...

1. Know FDRís platform ìtry somethingî and understand his actions to try and keep to this promise: rapid action in first 100 days, New Deal programs, fireside chats, court packing plan.

2. Identify some of the New Deal programs and explain how today they are still a part of American society: names of programs, what they did, and their purpose.

3. Understand and be able to explain the issues and opposing sides involved in Court Packing plan
 A. FDR: needed to secure the Nation.
 B. dissenters: programs unconstitutional.

4. Analyze and interpret meaning and symbolism in a variety of primary source documents, including pictures, political cartoons, and speeches.

5.  Research, interview, and write a well organized paper exploring social aspects of the Great Depression and the New Deal.
