First One-Hundred Days
Lecture with Primary Sources

Lecture Outline:
1. Intro:
 elected into a time of crisis, like Lincoln
 Inaugural address: ìAction and action now!î
 appointed liberals
      not afraid of bold reforms
      1st female cabinet member: Sec. of Labor Frances Perkins

2. Banking Crisis
 banks closing all over
 people afraid - ìrunsî on banks, withdraw $
 Emergency Banking Act (EBA)
     subject of first fireside chat (see below)
     banking holiday
     federal auditors examine banksí books
     only health banks allowed to re-open
     ìI assure you it is safer to keep your money in a bank than under your mattress.î - FDR
 early show of leadership
 1st of many bank reforms

3. The First Hundred Days
 New Deal  (Alphabet soup) quickly passed piece by piece
 15 major laws passed by an obedient, desperate congress
 Discuss Overhead: ìrapid actionî political cartoons and
       table (Use cartoon analysis to aid discussion.)
 spending bills passed -> govt spending = pump up economy
 3 Rís
      Relief: for people in need
      Recovery: employers and companies from depression
      Reform: long term prevention of repeat depression
 FDR very popular - re-elected 1936
 Long term effect: SSN, electricity, agricultural practices
       (paid not to farm), Wagner Act and unions, welfare state...
       *Find out about local New Deal programs still around!

4. Fireside Chats
 1st fireside chat (March 12) - explain EBA
 series of radio chats, from White House
 explain New Deal programs
 reassure worried people - felt President understood their problems
