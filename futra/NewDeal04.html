Town Meeting
Small Group Activity


Directions: Divide your students into groups of 4-5.  There is no limit on how many groups you can form.  On the board write (or do as a handout) the below list of people hurt by the Great Depression.  Ask each group to function as a town meeting from the Great Depression era. The purpose of the meeting to discuss how to help people who are being affected by the Great Depression.  Have them pretend that they are going to compose their ideas into a letter to the President Hoover.  Give the groups about a half an hour to brain storm and write their letter.  Then, a spokesperson from each group will read the letter in front of the rest of the class.  After each letter, comment on their ideas and point our their ideas that are similar to what FDR really did with his New Deal programs.
 

List of people to consider: (write on board)
1) Farmers who has lost their farms due to the dust bowl.
2) Unemployed, young men who are drifting around the country.
3) Elderly people who have no money to live on and can't find anyone willing to employ them.
4) Abused child laborers who must work to help their families, but receive small wages and work long, hard hours.
5) People who lost all their money when the banks closed down and when the stock market crashed.
6) Bankers who cannot reopen their banks due to a lack of funds and trusting customers.
7) Communities destroyed by the flooding rivers, which have flooded due to the practice of clear cutting.

Add any more to the list that you can think of.
