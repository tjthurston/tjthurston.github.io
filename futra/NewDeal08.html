Court Packing Plan
Class Reading and Discussion

Directions: Follow the below outline.  To add to this activity, I suggest finding a photo of FDR giving a Fire Side chat. You will want to make a class set of copies of the fireside chat.

Pre-Reading information:
 - Supreme Court feel FDR over step his rights as president
 declare AAA and NRA unconstitutional.
 - FDR not want Court to declare New Deal programs unconstitutional.
 - Fireside chat appeal to public about plan

Read document - Fireside Chat
 - call on a student to read a small section
 - then ask someone else to explain the small section just read
 - repeat until finished

Class discuss of document:
 - What was FDRís plan? (Over 70 years old -> new justice = 6, total of 15  justices if plan was implemented)
 - Why did he feel the need for the plan? Supreme Court out of  control, restore balance, protect publics wants and needs)

Post-reading information:
  - plan unpopular with public cause Supreme Court is to be separate  from parties and untouchable
 - plan didn't pass, some projects declared unconstitutional. (AAA, NRA) economy began to fail again

Related Cartoons

Short answer question for homework:
Take a side and support it with solid arguments.  It is 1937 and you are at a party in Washington D.C.  The conversation turns to President Franklin Roosevelt's Court Packing Plan.  Pretend you are either an aid for President Franklin Roosevelt, who supports the Court Packing Plan, or a dissenting Justices on the Supreme Court, who has been ruling against the New Deal programs.  Explain to the party guests what your stand is on the Court Pack Plan and why.
 

Source of Fireside Chat: Constitutional Law for a changing America 2nd ed., by Lee Epstein and Thomas Walker, Washington D.C.: Congressional Quarterly, Inc. 1995.
